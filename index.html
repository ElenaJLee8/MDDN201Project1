Internet Infrastructure of Wellington

Above our heads and beneath our feet is a network connecting us to the internet at any given moment in time.
Manhole covers, CCTV cameras, antennae, and markings on the street
are just some of the physical ways we can see the internet around us.

Manhole Covers

One of a few ways that we can get a glimpse of the networks below us, manhole covers come in many different sizes and designs. Pipe systems and a series of cables are hidden under these covers, sending data all over Wellington and connecting us all. They are usually marked with the names of the companies that own them, a few being Hynds Pipe Systems, Humes Pipeline Systems and the Wellington City Council.

CCTV Cameras

There are CCTV cameras all around us, ranging from ones to monitor traffic, theft prevention and personal security. We as individuals are captured in many of these cameras every day without even thinking about it. Unwittingly we become a part of a wider connected network.

Street Markings

At the moment around the Mt Cook and Newtown areas under ground work is being done, likely to be installing Fibre internet cables. Bright markings left on the footpaths are signs of the web of cables beneath our feet, and the people who put them there.

Antennae

On the tops of many of the buildings around us in the city are antennae of all different types. They are used primarily to receive television broadcasts in homes and workplaces.
